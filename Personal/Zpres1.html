1-Day Plan

Day 1

1. Understanding The Requierements
- Read the assignment carefully to understand what your presentation should be like.
- Use Elaborative Interrogation (ask yourself questions like: “Why is this topic important?”
and “What message do I want to convey?”) to deepen your understanding of the topic.

2. Research
- Gather information using credible sources.
- Summarize your research, via notes. If needed write down citations for reference.

3. Brainstorming
- Create a Mind Map to visually organize your ideas, identify key points, and establish connections.
- Group related ideas together to simplify complex information.

4. Outline
- While creating the outline use SQ3R Method (Survey, Question, Read, Recite, Review) in order to ensure logical flow.

5. Visual Material
- While creating visual materials remember to use Dual Coding (combine written notes with simple diagrams or visuals to reinforce understanding).

6. Practice
- Use Feynman Technique (explain your presentation out loud as if teaching someone unfamiliar with the topic).
- Use Self Explanation (reflect on why each part is important and how it connects to your overall message).
- Record yourself and review your presentation from playback.
- Adjust pacing, transitions, and slide content based on self-assessment.

Suggestion: Use the “Pomodoro Technique” in order to maximize your effectiveness.
