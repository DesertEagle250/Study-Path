Mindfulness


What is Mindfulness?

- Mindfulness is the practice of being fully present and aware of the current moment,
without judgment. It involves focusing on your thoughts, feelings, body sensations, and surroundings with a calm, accepting attitude.

Why Mindfulness Is Useful in Learning?

- Helps reduce distractions, enhancing concentration on the task.
- Encourages active engagement, improving information retention.
- Lowers anxiety, creating a calm mindset for effective learning.
- Helps recognize when focus drifts, allowing quick refocusing.

When to Use Mindfulness While Studying?

- Before Studying(to clear your mind and set intentions).
- During Study Breaks(to reset and maintain mental clarity).
- When Feeling Overwhelmed(to manage stress and regain focus).
- After Studying(to reflect and consolidate learning).

Suggested Mindfulness Technique: S.T.O.P Method

1. Stop:
- Pause your current activity.

2. Take a Breath:
- Inhale deeply through your nose, then exhale slowly through your mouth.
- Focus on the sensation of your breath entering and leaving your body.

3. Observe:
- Notice your current experience:
- Physical Sensations(scan your body for areas of tension or relaxation)
- Emotions(acknowledge any feelings present without judgment).
- Thoughts(be aware of your thoughts as they come and go).

4. Proceed:
- Resume your activity with a fresh sense of awareness and intention.
