1-Day Plan

Consider applying Mindfulness Techniques to your studies.

Click here to find out more about Mindfulness

Day 1

1. Understand the Exam Format
- Identify the types of questions (e.g., short answers, essays, problem-solving) and allowed materials (books, notes, formulas).
- Focus on application, analysis, and problem-solving, not just fact recall.

2. Organize Materials
- Decide materials by topic.
- Create quick-access tools: tab pages in notebooks, highlight
- Use Dual Coding (incorporate drawings and pictures into your notes) to reinforce understanding.

3. Revision
- Revise said notes till you know where to find what information.
- Make sure you understand the topic without looking at the notes.
- Focus on being able to apply said notes into real life scenarios.
- Review important concepts, revisiting them periodically.

4. Practice Questions
- Find questions that may come up on the exam or are similar to them.
- Answer practice questions without immediately looking at your notes. Check answers afterward to fill gaps

5.Review
- Check with the answers and correct mistakes if needed.

Suggestion: Use the “Pomodoro Technique” in order to maximize your effectiveness.
