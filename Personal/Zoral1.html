1-Day Plan

Consider applying Mindfulness Techniques to your studies.

Click here to find out more about Mindfulness

Day 1

1.Assessment
- Know the key topics, question types.
- Brake down the topic into smaller chunks.
- Use Elaborative Interrogation (ask yourself “Why is this topic important?” and) to stay motivated.

2.Notes
- Create notes from the syllabus.
- Use Dual Coding (incorporate drawings and pictures into your notes) to reinforce understanding.

3. Revision
- While studying use Self Explanation (explain the topic to yourself) to ensure the clarity and logic of each paragraph.
- Use The Pomodoro Technique to stay productive (break your work into 25-minute focused sessions with 5-minute breaks).
- Incorporate Active-Recall into this study session
- Revise your notes and try to remember as much as you can.
- Put them away.
- Write down everything you can remember from those notes.
- Check the result with your notes and see what you forgot.
- Repeat until you remember everything.
- Use Mnemonics (memory tricks that best suit your topic) for information that were challenging for you

4. Speaking Practice
- Use Feynman Technique (explain complex topics in simple language to ensure clarity).
- Practice answering possible exam questions without notes.
- Record yourself while answering those questions.

5. Review
- Review the recordings, note areas where you hesitated and work on them.
- Consider aspects like pacing and intonation.

Suggestion: Use the “Pomodoro Technique” in order to maximize your effectiveness.
