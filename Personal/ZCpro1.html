1-Day Plan

Day 1

1. Understanding
- Carefully read the instructions to understand what is expected (theme, format, materials).
- Use Elaborative Interrogation (ask yourself questions like: “Why is this project important?” and “What message do I want to convey?”).

2. Coming up with the Idea
- Break down the project into key components (e.g., concept, materials, design, execution).
- Use Mind Mapping to organize your ideas visually.
- Make rough sketches or outlines to visualize your ideas.

3. Research/Inspirations
- Search for inspiration from sources like art magazines, or art-focused websites.
- Take some brief notes so to easily refresh your memory while working on the project.

4. Planning
- Outline the steps needed to complete the project (design, build, review).
- Create a list of materials that you will need.
- Create a final sketch.

5. Creating
- Follow your plan.
- Use The Pomodoro Technique to stay productive (break your work into 25-minute focused sessions with 5-minute breaks).

6. Review and Refinement
- Step back and assess if the project meets the requirements without referring to the instructions.
- If possible, show it to someone for quick feedback.
- If needed make final adjustments according to the feedback and your own assessment.

Suggestion: Use the “Pomodoro Technique” in order to maximize your effectiveness.
